Undergraduate Research

Courey Lab

Department of Chemistry and Biochemistry, UCLA

January 2018 - Current

Undergraduate Student Researcher

We study the molecular mechanisms during development in our model organism of Drosophila melanogaster. My role in the lab has been to elucidate the spatiotemporal mechanism of SUMO within the Ras/MAPK pathway which has been previously shown to affect signaling in early Drosophila embryo development. I have designed and implemented purification strategies of covalently SUMO conjugated proteins and developed proteomic workflows to identify both SUMO conjugated proteins and specific covalently modified residues in order to have clear targets for site-directed mutagenesis of proteins involved in Ras signaling. Also, I have incorporated genetic and biochemical techniques to disrupt non-covalent interactions between SUMO and proteins involved in Ras signaling.

10-week Summer Research

Woo Lab

Department of Chemistry and Chemical Biology, Harvard University

June 2019 - August 2019

10-week Summer Researcher

We study th post-translation modification (PTM) and nutrient sensor, O-linked N-acetylglucosamine (O-GlcNAc), which modifies >3,000 proteins and is a key PTM that coordinates cellular responses determining cellular fate. O-GlcNAc Transferase (OGT) is the essential enzyme solely responsible for the addition of O-GlcNAc on proteins, but how OGT select its proteins to modify under certain stimuli is unknown. My role was to establish how OGT selects proteins to modify under heat stress. Over the summer, I applied chemo-enzymatic labeling and isotope targeted labeling proteomics to describe the O-GlcNAc proteome under heat stress in U2OS cells, where I was able to identify more O-GlcNAc proteins than what was known. I also implemented nanobody technology for proximity induced interaction between SUMO-conjugating enzyme-UBC9 and O-GlcNAc Transferase (OGT) for selective SUMO conjugation of OGT in U2OS and HEK 293T cells. Using immunofluorescence and fluorescence microscopy to I was able to visualize OGT localized to the cytoplasm with direct covalent SUMOylation and in contrast global increase of SUMOylated proteins under heat stress caused OGT to localize back into the nucleus. Current directions involves the disruption of motifs that allow for non-covalent interaction between OGT and SUMO as this protein-protein interaction might be used by OGT to appropriately modify proteins under heat stress
